Smart Features

7 Technologies That Make People Go "Whoa"

Each one is proven, affordable, and feeds directly into Lee's GHL CRM for automated follow-up.

1

Dynamic QR Codes Must Have

Not static QR codes — dynamic ones. The same printed QR code can be redirected to a different property page anytime. When the house sells, redirect it to the next listing or Lee's portfolio. One sign, infinite listings.

💡
Cool factor: Buyer scans QR → lands on a stunning property page with photos, virtual tour, price, and a "Schedule Viewing" button. Their phone number is captured before they even see the listing. Retarget them on Facebook/Google within hours.
2

NFC Tap-to-View Game Changer

Embed a £0.50 NFC chip behind the sign. Buyer holds their phone near the sign → instantly opens the listing page. No camera, no scanning, no typing. Just tap. Works on every iPhone and Android made since 2018.

📱
Cool factor: "TAP PHONE HERE" with a gold ring on the sign. People will literally walk up to try it. It feels like magic. HomeSmart just launched this in May 2025 and it's their biggest differentiator.
3

SMS Text Code Lead Machine

TEXT "DEAL" TO 813-675-0916 — buyer texts the code, instantly receives property details via SMS. Behind the scenes: their phone number is captured in GHL, tagged with the property address, and an automated follow-up sequence begins. Works for people who don't know how to scan QR codes (older demographic).

💬
Cool factor: Each property gets a unique text code (DEAL1, DEAL2, etc). The reply includes a photo, price, beds/baths, and a link. The lead is in your CRM before they've even driven past the house.
4

Solar LED Illumination 24/7 Visibility

Solar-powered LED spotlights clip onto the sign post. The sign glows at night — 200% more visibility after sunset. Most buyers drive neighborhoods after work (5-8pm). In Cleveland winters, that's pitch dark. Your sign is the only one they can read.

🌟
Cool factor: Use cyan LED (#00E5FF) to match the brand. The sign literally glows in Lee's signature neon blue. Unmistakable. Cost: ~$25-40 per solar light unit, rechargeable, weatherproof.
03 / 08
Smart Features (continued)

Three More Tricks Nobody Else Does

5

AR Virtual Tour Trigger Future-Proof

The QR code doesn't just open a webpage — it opens an AR experience. Buyer points their phone at the house and sees floating info: price, bed/bath count, square footage, and a "Walk Inside" button that launches a 360° virtual tour. They're touring the house from the sidewalk.

🏠
Cool factor: Goldman Sachs projects the AR real estate market at $2.6B by 2025. Early adopters win. This makes Lee look like he's from the future while competitors are still using paper flyers.
6

Retargeting Pixel on Landing Page Ninja Move

Every QR/NFC landing page has Facebook Pixel + Google Tag. When someone scans the sign, they start seeing DDWL ads on Facebook, Instagram, and Google within hours. The sign follows them home. Even if they don't call, they see Lee's brand everywhere for the next 30 days.

🎯
Cool factor: A $5/day retargeting budget means every person who scanned any DDWL sign sees Lee's face and listings on their phone for a month. That's maybe 100 impressions per lead for pennies.
7

"Sold" Sign That Keeps Working Secret Weapon

When a property sells, don't remove the sign immediately. Flip the rider to "SOLD BY LEE" and redirect the dynamic QR code to show: "This home sold in X days. Want to sell yours? Want to see similar listings?" The sign keeps generating leads even after the deal closes.

🔥
Cool factor: Neighbors see the "SOLD" sign, scan it out of curiosity, and land on a page that says "Your neighbor's home sold for $X in Y days. Wondering what yours is worth?" with a home valuation form. That's how you farm an entire neighborhood from one sign.

Lead Flow

How Every Sign Feeds Into GHL

This is the real magic. Every interaction with a sign — scan, tap, or text — creates a contact in GoHighLevel and triggers an automated sequence.

1

Buyer Interacts with Sign

Scans QR, taps NFC, or texts SMS code. All three methods work.

2

Landing Page Captures Lead

Property page loads with photos, details, virtual tour. Facebook Pixel + Google Tag fire. If SMS: phone number auto-captured. If QR/NFC: lead form captures name + phone before showing full details.

3

Contact Created in GHL

Webhook fires → new contact in GHL. Tagged with: property address, source (sign), interaction type (QR/NFC/SMS), date/time.

4

Automated Follow-Up Begins

Immediately: SMS with property details + Lee's intro.
1 hour: Email with virtual tour link + similar listings.
24 hours: SMS "Still interested? I can get you in for a showing today."
3 days: Email with market report for that neighborhood.
7 days: "Properties are moving fast" urgency SMS.

5

Retargeting Ads Activate

Within hours, the lead sees DDWL ads on Facebook, Instagram, and Google. Lee's face, the property, and similar listings follow them everywhere for 30 days. Cost: ~$0.01-0.05 per impression.
